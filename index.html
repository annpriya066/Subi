The size of an atom or ion and the attraction between the nucleus and the outermost electrons play important roles in determining the chemistry of an element. Knowing the trends in atomic and ionic sizes, ionization energies, and electron negativity aids in understanding chemical behavior and the nature of chemical bonds.



Learning Objective


Know periodic trends of atomic size, ionic size, ionization energy, and electron negativity

Success Criteria


Be able to predict size differences among atoms and ions
Be able to predict differences in ionization energy and electron negativity among elements.
Be able to predict relative reactivity of related elements.
